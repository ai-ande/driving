How people drive

Seconds before a stopped driver follows the car ahead (or the green). This is why a queue “unzips” one car at a time.

Time gap kept behind the car ahead while moving.

How briskly people accelerate back to speed.

Low: stare at the bumper ahead, brake late and hard. High: read the road far ahead, ease off early, arrive at lights still rolling.

Share of stopped drivers on their phone when their turn comes — each miss adds 1–4 s of dead green and punches a hole in the queue. This is why the back of a queue often never moves before the light turns red again.

Overrides that share of cars with a trained driver (0.3 s reaction, 1.0 s cushion, coasts instead of braking). Watch what even 20% does.

The lights

Full red-to-red cycle at every intersection.

Big cross streets get the rest. Minor crossings (Toomey, Riverside, 9th, 10th) take a short fixed phase — they stop Lamar even when empty.

Cycle time at major crossings (Barton Springs, Cesar Chavez, 5th, 6th, 12th) that neither through movement gets — protected left arrows, pedestrian phases. The city’s real splits are pending a records request; this is the generic reality tax.

Offsets each light so a platoon leaving one green arrives at the next one. Timed northbound at the speed above — note what it does to southbound.

Reading the map

  • car color = speed (red stopped → blue at the limit)
  • red halo = foot on the brake
  • amber ring = green light / gap open, driver hasn’t reacted yet
  • 5th St runs one-way east, 6th St one-way west — Austin’s downtown couplet.

v0.3 — through traffic only (no turns yet; real 5th & Lamar also loses a lane to right-turners yielding at the crosswalk). Signals are two-phase plus a generic turn-arrow/walk allowance at majors — the city’s actual plans are being requested. 3rd St is unsignalized and 15th St passes over Lamar — both verified against the city signal registry.
