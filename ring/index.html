The road

The 2008 experiment used 22 cars on a 230 m circle. More cars = less room to absorb mistakes.

Trained: keeps a generous cushion, reads several cars ahead, never over-brakes — spends the gap to absorb the wave instead of passing it on amplified.

How the humans drive

How stale the picture in each driver's head is. Lag is the engine of phantom jams: you respond to where traffic was.

Time gap behind the leader. Small cushions leave nothing to absorb with.

Random drift in speed-keeping — glances at the phone, the radio, the dog.

What to watch

  • Start: everyone cruising. Within a couple of minutes, a jam forms from nothing and orbits backward.
  • Click Convert ONE driver: the white-dot car opens a cushion when it senses the wave breathing ahead, and softens it — hard braking drops immediately.
  • Slide trained drivers up: 4 of 28 cuts hard braking by ~65%. Everyone trained: glass-smooth. And try FEWER cars — below ~19 this ring can’t jam at all: density and behavior both matter.

Physics: Intelligent Driver Model + per-driver perception lag and noise.
