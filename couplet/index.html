What to watch

  • Uncoordinated: both systems limp equally — geometry doesn't matter when timing is random.
  • Coordinated: the arterial can only give its wave to one direction — northbound flies, southbound still stops. The pair gives both directions a perfect wave, because each street only has one direction to please.
  • This is thesis belief #7c — and it's why Austin's 5th/6th (and hundreds of downtown pairs) exist. Modern cities sometimes convert them back for walkability; flow is only one good among several.

Physics: IDM + startup reaction + fixed-time signals; no cross traffic spawned (the cross phase still steals time, as in life). Identical driver population and light hardware on both sides — only the geometry differs.
